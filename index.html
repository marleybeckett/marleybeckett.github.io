I am currently working towards my Master of Applied Science in Electrical Engineering at the UBC. My studies are focused in the areas of wireless communications protocols, low power IoT, and machine learning.

About Me

I am a passionate individual who thrives on curiosity, always eager to uncover the "why" and "how." My refusal to settle for the status quo drives me to push boundaries and explore new horizons.


Currently, I am pursuing my Master's degree at UBC focusing on the fusion of in electrical and computer engineering, specifically exploring low-power, low-complexity, backscattering IoT devices. As a proud recipient of the NSERC Canadian Graduate Scholarship for Master’s (CGS M), I actively collaborate with industry professionals and participate in 3GPP meetings to bring cohesion to this multifaceted domain.


My academic journey began with a B.Sc. in Electrical Engineering from Queen’s University (’21), where my dedication to community involvement, academic excellence, and innovative thinking earned me the Chancellor Scholarship.


Throughout my university education, I have combined academic and athletic pursuits. I have competed in Varsity Cross Country and Track and Field, securing three national championship titles. You will often find me immersed in the vibrant outdoor scene of Vancouver. cruising through local trails on my mountain bike, skiing the local mountains, or catching the sunset from summits, I believe in making every moment count. Whether delving into the wilderness in my backyard or embarking on a four-month trek from Mexico to Canada, the idea of "what else is out there" is always on my mind.
