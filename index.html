About Me

I am constantly seeking to understand the "why" and "how" behind everything. I thrive on the excitement of pushing boundaries and venturing into new territories. Currently, you'll find me knee-deep in my Master's program at UBC, where I'm on a mission to revolutionize wireless communication protocols for the next generation of backscattering-based IoT devices (think RFID, but supercharged). Thanks to the NSERC Canadian Graduate Scholarship for Master’s (CGS M) and the Mitacs Accelerate Fellowship, I'm not just studying - I'm collaborating with industry professionals and even contributing to 3GPP meetings.

Before UBC, I completed my Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering at Queen’s University (’21), where I was fortunate to be awarded the Chancellor Scholarship for my academic success and community involvement.

When I'm not deep into my studies, I'm most likely running or exploring the outdoors; it's where I recharge and find my balance. From spiking up and seeing how fast I can run 12.5 laps around a track to epic treks from Mexico to Canada, I'm always chasing that next big adventure and wondering, "What else is out there?"

Competative Running

I'd be lying if I didn't say that running is a huge part of my life. Over the past 10 years, it has taken up more of my daily life than a part-time job. When I first started at Queen's in 2017, I walked on to the cross-country team. It wasn't until my third year that I truly found my stride. In 2019, I proudly assumed the role of team captain, leading us to the University's first national title. Despite a hiatus from running prompted by the challenges of a global pandemic, I returned to competing and training when I started my Master's at UBC. This past year, I became a National Champion for not only the second, but third time. I have never felt more myself than I do after an easy run through Pacific Spirit Park with my teammates or red-lining during track workouts.
